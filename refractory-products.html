Refractory Products.
Insulation, fibres & fired shapes.

A complementary line of formed refractory products — calcium silicate boards and pipe sections, engineered ceramic fibre, and a complete range of fired refractory shapes across silicon carbide, nitride bonded SiC, mullite, high alumina, zirconia–mullite, and insulation grades. Lining systems for kilns, furnaces, ladles, reactors, and reduction cells from 650 °C through 1800 °C.

Eight product types across three families: calcium silicate insulation, engineered ceramic fibre, and six fired refractory chemistries.

— 01 / Refractory Products

Calcium Silicate Board & Pipes

A green-generation thermal insulation material engineered for high-temperature service. Lightweight, asbestos-free, water-resistant, and dimensionally stable — calcium silicate boards and pipe sections cover the 650 °C to 1100 °C envelope, with ultra-high-density and nano-grade variants pushing further still.

Used across power plants, petrochemical refineries, steel mills, kiln walls, oven linings, fire doors, and process pipework where the lining must hold its shape, shed water, and stay non-combustible across thousands of thermal cycles.

— 01 / Silicon Carbide

Silicon Carbide Refractories

Maximum thermal conductivity. Slag-resistant. Built for the hottest service.

Silicon carbide refractories are produced from synthetic SiC grain — formed by reacting high-purity silica with carbon in resistance furnaces above 2,400 °C — bonded with engineered ceramic systems and fired into precision shapes. The result is a refractory body with thermal conductivity an order of magnitude higher than fireclay, exceptional resistance to thermal shock, and chemical inertness across most slag and flame environments. Available in clay-bonded, oxide-bonded, and self-bonded systems, formed by cold-isostatic pressing, slip casting, or extrusion depending on the geometry required.

Key Properties

  • High thermal conductivity for rapid heat transfer and uniform thermal profiles
  • Excellent hot strength — modulus of rupture retained at service temperatures
  • Outstanding thermal shock resistance under repeated cycling
  • High abrasion and erosion resistance against particulate-laden flue streams
  • Chemical inertness to most acidic slags, flame impingement, and reducing atmospheres
  • Capability for complex precision shapes through advanced forming and finishing

Forming Capabilities

Pressed shapes, slip-cast hollow geometries, isostatically pressed close-tolerance components, machined bodies for tight dimensional control, and large-format custom shapes to drawing.

— 02 / Nitride Bonded Silicon Carbide

Nitride Bonded Silicon Carbide Refractories

Oxidation-resistant SiC. Engineered for non-ferrous metal contact.

Nitride bonded silicon carbide refractories combine silicon carbide grain with a silicon nitride (Si₃N₄) bonding phase, formed by nitriding silicon-bearing precursors in a controlled nitrogen atmosphere at high temperature. The nitride bond produces a refractory with significantly improved oxidation resistance over conventional SiC, while preserving the high thermal conductivity, thermal shock resistance, and mechanical strength that make silicon carbide the material of choice for thermally aggressive service. Their non-wetting behaviour against molten non-ferrous metals makes them particularly valuable in aluminium, copper, zinc, and galvanizing applications.

Key Properties

  • Very high thermal conductivity, comparable to standard SiC grades
  • Superior oxidation resistance — extends service life in oxidizing atmospheres
  • High hot strength, retained at extreme temperatures
  • Excellent thermal shock resistance from moderate thermal expansion
  • Strong erosion and abrasion resistance against molten metal and gas-borne particulates
  • Chemical inertness to non-ferrous metals, alkalis, and many slag systems
  • Non-wetting surface chemistry — resists metal penetration and dross adherence

— 03 / Mullite

Mullite Refractories

Thermal stability under cycling. The kiln-furniture standard.

Mullite refractories are formulated around the 3Al₂O₃·2SiO₂ phase — a stoichiometric aluminosilicate that crystallizes during firing and confers exceptional thermal stability, low thermal expansion, and good creep resistance at sustained high temperatures. Furner produces mullite shapes from controlled blends of synthetic mullite, fused mullite, and high-purity alumino-silicate raw materials, fired to develop a continuous mullite matrix with well-controlled porosity. The result is a refractory body that holds its dimensions through thousands of thermal cycles, resists deformation under load at high temperature, and shows minimal reactivity with most kiln atmospheres.

Key Properties

  • Excellent thermal shock resistance through low coefficient of thermal expansion
  • High creep resistance — minimal deformation under sustained load and temperature
  • Good chemical resistance to mildly acidic and neutral atmospheres
  • Stable phase composition up to operating temperatures around 1700 °C
  • Low thermal conductivity relative to silicon carbide — useful where thermal isolation matters
  • Dimensional stability across thousands of firing cycles

— 04 / High Alumina

High Alumina Refractories

Wide-spectrum refractory chemistry. Tuned by alumina content.

High alumina refractories cover a continuum of compositions from approximately 50% to 99% Al₂O₃, with each step up the alumina ladder bringing higher refractoriness, improved corrosion resistance, and greater mechanical strength at temperature. Furner manufactures high alumina shapes across the full range — from bauxite-based 60–70% grades through tabular and white fused alumina-based 90%+ premium grades — fired to dense, low-porosity bodies engineered for specific service conditions. Composition is matched to application: bauxite chemistries for general high-temperature service, tabular alumina for thermal shock and slag resistance, and fused alumina grades for the most aggressive corrosion and abrasion environments.

Key Properties

  • Refractoriness scaling with alumina content — service temperatures up to ~1800 °C in premium grades
  • High mechanical strength and load-bearing capacity at elevated temperatures
  • Strong resistance to acidic, neutral, and many basic slag systems
  • Good thermal shock resistance, particularly in tabular alumina grades
  • Low porosity bodies for reduced slag penetration and longer campaign life
  • Versatile across reducing, oxidizing, and neutral atmospheres

— 05 / Zirconia–Mullite

Zirconia–Mullite Refractories

Toughened ceramic. Engineered against thermal shock.

Zirconia–mullite refractories incorporate zirconia (ZrO₂) into a mullite matrix, exploiting the transformation toughening behaviour of zirconia to dramatically improve thermal shock resistance and crack arrest. The dispersed zirconia phase undergoes controlled tetragonal-to-monoclinic transformation under thermal stress, absorbing crack-tip energy and significantly extending service life under cyclic conditions. Furner produces zirconia–mullite shapes for applications where rapid heating and cooling rates would shorten the campaign of conventional alumino-silicates — investment casting shells, glass tank components, and high-temperature insulation backup layers.

Key Properties

  • Outstanding thermal shock resistance through transformation toughening
  • High refractoriness with stable phase composition up to ~1700 °C
  • Excellent corrosion resistance against molten glass and acidic slag systems
  • Good mechanical strength retained across thermal cycles
  • Low thermal expansion compared to pure alumina-based bodies
  • Resistance to glass-line cutting and erosion

— 06 / Insulation

Insulation Refractories

Engineered porosity. Heat retained where it should be.

Insulation refractories are engineered for low thermal conductivity — high-porosity refractory bodies designed to reduce shell temperatures, lower fuel consumption, and shorten heat-up cycles in industrial furnaces and kilns. Furner produces insulation refractories across the full range of operating temperatures, from low-density backup insulation for moderate service to high-strength insulation refractories rated for direct hot-face exposure at temperatures up to ~1700 °C. Each grade is formulated around a specific balance of porosity, mechanical strength, and refractoriness, with bulk densities engineered to match the thermal duty.

Key Properties

  • Low thermal conductivity for energy-efficient furnace operation
  • Reduced bulk density — lighter loads on furnace structures, faster thermal response
  • Engineered porosity for optimized insulation while maintaining usable strength
  • Refractoriness graded to service — from 1100 °C to 1700 °C operating ranges
  • Compatible with both hot-face and backup insulation layouts
  • Available in standard brick formats and custom shapes for transitions and arches

— Built to specification

Every shape, fired to your drawing.

Furner manufactures fired refractories as standard catalogue shapes and as custom-engineered components to customer drawings. Forming routes include uniaxial and isostatic pressing, slip casting, extrusion, and hand-moulding for large-format shapes. Firing is conducted in shuttle kilns and tunnel kilns under controlled atmospheres — oxidizing, neutral, or nitriding as the chemistry requires. Every batch is verified against full physical and chemical testing: bulk density, apparent porosity, cold crushing strength, modulus of rupture, refractoriness under load, permanent linear change on reheating, and full chemical analysis. Specifications can be matched to ASTM, IS, DIN, or customer-specific standards.
